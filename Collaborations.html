Collaborations

I am interested in collaborations at the interface of cancer biology, metastasis, tumor metabolism, tumor microenvironment, and multi-omics analysis. My current work focuses on how metastatic cancer cells adapt to the bone niche, particularly in prostate cancer bone metastasis.

I welcome collaborations with researchers in computational biology, bioinformatics, biostatistics, tumor immunology, cancer metabolism, metastasis biology, and bone microenvironment research. I am especially interested in projects that connect data-driven discovery with biological interpretation and experimental validation.

What I Am Interested In

  • Computational biology, bioinformatics, biostatistics, and machine learning for cancer data analysis
  • Multi-omics integration — RNA-seq, single-cell RNA-seq, ATAC-seq, spatial profiling, metabolomics, proteomics, and CRISPR screen datasets
  • Metastasis research across cancer types, especially prostate cancer, breast cancer, and bone-tropic tumors
  • Bone metastasis and tumor–bone crosstalk — mechanisms of bone colonization and the bone metastatic microenvironment
  • Tumor metabolism, mitochondrial stress, metabolic plasticity, and stress-adaptive programs
  • Tumor immunobiology and immunometabolism — immune suppression, macrophage biology, and T cell dysfunction

What I Can Contribute

  • Biological interpretation of cancer genomics and multi-omics datasets
  • Expertise in bone biology, bone metastasis, tumor metabolism, and mitochondrial stress biology
  • CRISPR functional genomics and screen interpretation
  • Experimental design for mechanistic validation
  • Ex vivo and in vivo metastasis model development
  • Grant, manuscript, and collaborative project development
