The adverse effects of chemotherapy on bone mass are not prevented by senolytics

Md Mohsin Ali, Pilar Simmons, Aaron Warren, Landon B. Gatrell, Ana Resende-Coelho, Taylor McElroy, Antiño R. Allen, Maria Almeida
Scientific Reports 15 (1), 17279
Summary:

Chemotherapy causes persistent bone loss, but senolytic drugs—which target senescent cells—do not prevent this damage. This work challenges the assumption that therapy-induced senescent cells are the primary driver of bone loss and suggests multi-target approaches are needed.

Paper thumbnail

Mechanisms of mitochondrial reactive oxygen species action in bone mesenchymal cells

Md Mohsin Ali, Intawat Nookaew, Ana Resende-Coelho, Adriana Marques-Carvalho, Aaron Warren, Qiang Fu, Ha-Neui Kim, Charles A. O'Brien, Maria Almeida
bioRxiv (preprint)
Summary:

Using single-cell RNA-seq, we show that mitochondrial reactive oxygen species (ROS) in osteoblast-lineage cells disrupts multiple pathways including PTH signaling, iron metabolism, and proteostasis—but does not drive cellular senescence, suggesting alternative mechanisms drive age-related bone loss.

Paper thumbnail

Mitochondrial oxidative stress or decreased autophagy in osteoblast lineage cells is not sufficient to mimic the deleterious effects of aging on bone mechanoresponsiveness

Ana Resende-Coelho, Md Mohsin Ali, Alicen James, Aaron Warren, Landon Gatrell, Ilham Kadhim, Qiang Fu, Jinhu Xiong, Melda Onal, Maria Almeida
Aging 17 (3), 610
Summary:

This study examined whether inducing mitochondrial ROS or suppressing autophagy in bone cells can recapitulate aging-like bone dysfunction. Results indicate that these individual stressors are insufficient, pointing to multi-factorial mechanisms of age-related bone loss.
