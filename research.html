Research

My research spans tumor metabolism, the metastatic microenvironment, and skeletal biology. I use functional genomics, metabolomics, and in vivo mouse models to ask mechanistic questions with translational potential.

Tumor Metabolism & the Metastatic Niche (Current)

Investigating how castration-resistant prostate cancer (CRPC) cells metabolically reprogram the pre-metastatic microenvironment. I use integrated metabolomic profiling and functional genomics to identify metabolic vulnerabilities in metastatic CRPC that could be therapeutically targeted.

Immunometabolism & Epigenetics

Studying how metabolic reprogramming in immune cells within the tumor microenvironment alters epigenetic landscapes and immune function. This work aims to uncover mechanisms that drive immune evasion in aggressive cancers and inform combination immunotherapy strategies.

CRISPR Screening for Cancer Vulnerabilities

Applying genome-wide and targeted CRISPR screens to discover synthetic lethal interactions and essential metabolic genes in metastatic cancer cells, connecting functional genomics to potential drug targets.

Mitochondrial ROS & Skeletal Aging (PhD Work)

Showed that mitochondrial oxidative stress in osteoblast-lineage cells disrupts PTH signaling, iron metabolism, and proteostasis through single-cell RNA-seq analysis — but does not drive cellular senescence as previously assumed. This supports multi-target combination approaches for age-associated osteoporosis.

Chemotherapy-Induced Bone Loss (PhD Work)

Demonstrated that standard breast-cancer chemotherapy (doxorubicin + cyclophosphamide + docetaxel) causes persistent bone loss up to 12 months after treatment cessation. Importantly, senolytic drugs did not prevent this damage, challenging the assumption that therapy-induced senescent cells are the primary driver.
